# Leviticus — Doctrine Layer

**Book:** Leviticus  
**Series:** Goldstonian Concordance Bible (GCB)  
**Doctrine:** Mirror → Water → Fire Doctrine  

## Mirror
Leviticus shows the truth of God as holy and near, yet never casual. Access to His presence requires sacrifice, mediation, purity, and covenant obedience. Sin is costly, blood matters, and worship cannot be self-defined.

## Water
Leviticus cleanses distorted ideas of worship by restoring ordered approach to God. It teaches that holiness reaches into food, body, sexuality, justice, priesthood, festivals, and land. The whole life is washed into covenant order.

## Fire
Leviticus refines through sacrifice, priestly discipline, the Day of Atonement, the judgment of Nadab and Abihu, and the sanctions of covenant blessing and curse. Divine holiness burns away careless religion.

## Goldstonian Reading
Within the Goldstonian Concordance Bible (GCB), Leviticus is the canonical handbook of holy proximity. It should be read not as obsolete ritual detail, but as the theology of access, mediation, cleansing, sacred order, and consecrated life before the presence of God.
