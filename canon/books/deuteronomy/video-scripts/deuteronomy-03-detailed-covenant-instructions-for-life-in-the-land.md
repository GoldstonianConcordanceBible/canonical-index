## Long-Form Script
Deuteronomy now moves from covenant principles to covenant practice. If Israel is going to live in the land as God's people, then worship, leadership, justice, and daily life must all be structured by the word of God. This section shows that covenant is not limited to private devotion. It shapes public civilization.

Worship must be protected from corruption. The people are warned against false prophets, false worship, and religious imitation of the nations. Holy place, holy practice, and holy distinction matter because syncretism destroys covenant identity from within.

Economic life also comes under covenant command. Debt release, generosity, gleaning, fair wages, honest measures, and care for the poor all reveal that obedience is social. Deuteronomy refuses a split between worship and justice.

Leadership is also bounded. Judges, priests, Levites, and even the future king must remain under the law. Power does not stand above covenant. This is one of Deuteronomy's most important political truths: leadership is legitimate only when it stays under the word of God.

Family, sexuality, warfare, refuge, testimony, and neighbor duty are all addressed. The result is a picture of covenant life that is deeply practical. Holiness must live in homes, courts, fields, armies, and assemblies.

Within this section also appears the promise of a prophet like Moses. This is one of Deuteronomy's clearest forward-looking moments. Moses will die, but God will continue to speak. The covenant future is not silent after Moses.

Under the Mirror → Water → Fire Doctrine, this section becomes highly concrete.

Mirror: it reveals that covenant life must govern institutions, not just private spirituality.  
Water: it cleanses social disorder by bringing worship, justice, leadership, and mercy under divine instruction.  
Fire: it refines by confronting corruption, false worship, abuse of power, and careless community life.

This is why Deuteronomy 12 through 26 matters. It is the architecture of covenant civilization in the land. It shows what remembered obedience looks like when it becomes public life.

## Closing Line
Deuteronomy teaches that if the land is to remain holy, then worship, justice, leadership, and daily life must all stay under the word of God.
