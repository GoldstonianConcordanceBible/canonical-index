# Exodus — Long-Form Script

**Series:** Goldstonian Concordance Bible (GCB)  
**Canon:** 81-Book Ethiopian Canon  
**Doctrine:** Mirror → Water → Fire Doctrine  

## Scripture Lead
“I am the LORD thy God, which have brought thee out of Egypt.” — Exodus 20:2

## Book Thesis
Exodus is the book of deliverance, covenant formation, and divine presence. It reveals how God redeems a people from bondage, forms them through law and worship, and dwells among them in holiness.

## Full Summary
Exodus begins in suffering. The descendants of Jacob, who entered Egypt under Joseph's preservation, have multiplied into a people. But a new Pharaoh rises who does not remember Joseph, and Israel is subjected to oppression, forced labor, and state-sponsored death. The opening of Exodus establishes one of the great biblical truths: the people of God may be under pressure, but they are never outside the sight of God.

Into this setting Moses is born, preserved, exiled, and eventually called. At the burning bush, God reveals Himself not as a distant principle but as the covenant God who sees, hears, remembers, and comes down to deliver.
