# Episode 10 — Choose This Day

**Scripture Lead:** “Choose this day whom you will serve.” — Joshua 24:15

## Opening Hook
Joshua ends not with ambiguity, but with decision.

At Shechem, he recounts covenant history from Abraham onward and then forces the issue:
Who will you serve?

This is not a motivational slogan.
It is a covenant courtroom.

## Core Teaching
Joshua 24 gathers memory into verdict.

The people must not merely admire what God has done. They must answer it. Joshua places before them the gods beyond the River, the gods of the Amorites, and the living God who brought them out and carried them into the land.

That is the final test:
when history is recited clearly, what allegiance will follow?

Joshua’s own declaration is among the most famous in Scripture:
**“As for me and my house, we will serve the LORD.”**

But the chapter is even deeper than personal resolve. It establishes public witness, covenant testimony, and memorial confirmation.

The people answer.
Joshua warns them.
The covenant is renewed.
A stone stands as witness.

## Mirror
This chapter mirrors the human tendency to enjoy religious language without decisive allegiance.

Joshua does not allow admiration without submission.

## Water
Water here is the cleansing power of remembered history.

The people are washed through rehearsal of what God has done, so their choice will not be made in ignorance.

## Fire
Fire is decision under witness.

To choose is to be refined.
To refuse neutrality is to pass through fire.
This chapter burns away the fantasy that covenant can coexist indefinitely with rival loyalties.

## Canonical Arc
Joshua 24 closes the book by showing that the issue was never merely land.
It was worship.

That is why the book remains enduringly relevant.
Every generation eventually stands at its own Shechem.

## Closing Exhortation
You cannot live forever on inherited memory.

At some point, covenant history confronts you personally.
Not just what your fathers believed.
Not just what your community says.
Whom will you serve?

Choose this day.
